Aquatic Invasive Species

Invasive species pose significant threats to Algonquin Park's ecosystems, particularly our freshwater species like Brook Trout. Aquatic invasive species have been detected in the park, resulting in permanent damage to food webs. Remember, as visitors to the park on visitor roads are close to recreational areas.

Please do your part to protect Algonquin Park's unique aquatic life species:

  • clean, drain, and dry all camping, boating, and angling gear before arriving to Algonquin Park
  • inspect and remove all plants, animals, or mud from boating and angling gear before travelling to another waterbody
  • never move fish or other aquatic species between waterbodies
  • the use of live baitfish is prohibited
  • angling and and aquatic invasive species to park staff or Fisheries or call
